What's wrong with the current site

Nine issues, ordered by impact. Each one explained in plain language, with the technical detail below.

Doesn't work well on mobile

Critical

Over 60% of people browsing the web are on their phone. If your site feels clunky or broken on a phone. Buttons too small to tap, images misaligned, text hard to read. They leave within seconds and order from someone else.

Platform template with limited mobile-first CSS control. Tap targets inconsistent with 44px minimum. Menu images and category navigation don't reflow cleanly below 768px. No responsive typography system. Lighthouse mobile score estimated ~40.

The site loads too slowly

Critical

The current site takes several seconds just to show anything. Most visitors give up after 3 seconds, and slow sites also rank lower on Google. Which makes the discoverability problem worse.

Weebly/EditMySite ships large render-blocking JS bundles before any content paints. Images served without WebP/AVIF or lazy loading. No first-party CDN. Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS) likely failing Google thresholds.

Invisible on Google

Critical

When someone searches "French pastry London Ontario" or "best bakery near me," your site doesn't show up. New customers can't find you no matter how good the pastries are. They go to whoever does show up first.

No structured data (JSON-LD for Bakery / LocalBusiness schema). JS-rendered site behind Cloudflare blocks direct crawlers. Title tags and meta descriptions are platform defaults. No sitemap.xml submitted to Google Search Console.

The design doesn't match the pastries

High

The pastries are exquisite. Lamination, plating, packaging, everything. The website, unfortunately, looks like a generic bakery template. Walking into the shop feels premium; visiting the website doesn't. That gap costs first-time online customers who decide based on vibe alone.

Generic platform theme with no brand customization. No display-serif typography pairing. No editorial layout or whitespace discipline. Zero micro-interactions (hover states, scroll animations). Colour palette and type system don't carry through the French-Japanese identity.

No clear path to order or act

High

A visitor lands on the homepage. What do you want them to do? Order online? Pre-order a cake? See the menu? Visit the shop? Right now, nothing tells them. Without a clear next step, people just close the tab.

No persistent nav CTA for ordering. No hero conversion button above the fold. Primary action buried in secondary links. Phone number not tel: linked. No contact form. No lead capture or newsletter prominence.

Your beautiful photos aren't used well

High

Your product photography is genuinely great. Natural light, careful plating, real craft. But the site shows images small, in grids, without story or scale. Pastries this good deserve editorial treatment: big hero shots, close-ups, the signature branded packaging celebrated.

No hero image, no gallery page, no lightbox or zoom interaction. Product images displayed at uniform thumbnail size. Branded packaging (tiramisu label, cake boxes) not featured visually anywhere. No Instagram feed surfaced on site.

Hours & location are buried

Medium

You're only open 5 days a week. People coming to the site to check if they can swing by for a croissant shouldn't have to hunt for hours. Same with the Piccadilly Street address and phone number. Those should be one glance away, every page.

Hours not in persistent footer or header. No OpeningHoursSpecification schema. Map embed inconsistent. Phone number not tel: linked. No closed-day visual indicator. Address shown on only a single page.

Won't appear in AI search results

Medium

When someone asks ChatGPT, Claude, or Google Gemini to recommend the best patisserie in London, Ontario, your business doesn't come up. AI tools only pull from websites they can actually read. And right now, yours is hard for them to read.

AI search engines (ChatGPT Browse, Perplexity, Google SGE, Claude) index via structured crawling. Current site's JS-rendered content + Cloudflare challenge blocks most automated readers. No JSON-LD entity signals or crawlable structured content.

The platform limits what's possible

Medium

Being locked into a template platform means every future idea, a seasonal campaign, a better gallery, a new landing page, anything unique to Saisha's, has to squeeze into someone else's box. A proper custom site grows with the business instead of constraining it.

Weebly/EditMySite template constraints prevent custom components, animations, or page types. Limited control over head tags, robots.txt, redirects. Locked into recurring platform fees. No way to use modern build tools or integrate custom APIs (e.g. Square Orders API natively).

What's working

A few things worth keeping, they transfer directly to the new site.

A strong, distinctive brand

Did Well

"Saisha's Pâtisserie" with the French-Japanese fusion angle is memorable, specific, and hard to copy. The script logo and signature packaging are elegant. This is a great foundation.

Exceptional product photography

Did Well

The product photos are beautiful. Natural light, clean plating, real pastries. The new site gives them the room they deserve.

Outstanding Google reviews (4.8★)

Did Well

123+ reviews averaging 4.8 stars is rare for any small business. The customer love is there, the new site just surfaces it.
